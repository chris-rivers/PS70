<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>Week 5: 3D Design & Printing</h3> <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <a id="btn" href="wk5.zip" download>Download my files from this week! </a> </div> <p class="margin"> </p> <h4>Assignment: Design and Print Something</h4> This week we started by having to 3D print something. For this assignment I wanted to make one of those little gyroscope fidget toy thingies. I’ve seen them be 3D printed before (there are some examples in the lab, and it’s not something easily made with subtractive methods. The basic idea was to make concentric rings that would themselves increase in diameter as you got further up on their z axis. The increase in diameter would need to be such that at the center z point of any given ring where it’s the widest, it is wider than the ring around it. At first I wasn’t really sure how to actually model this. I was used to making things from a top down perspective, where I would sketch them from the top and then just extrude the geometries to the depths they needed to be. This wasn’t possible here, because the rings needed to be extruded at variable depths along the axes. Instead, the best way I found to accomplish the desired shape was actually to model just the radial cross section of all the rings, and then pivot that cross section around the center of the rings. <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <img src="week5sketch.png"> </div> <p class="caption">The radial cross section of the rings…</p> <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <img src="week5body" > </div> <p class="caption"> …Pivoted around the center!</p> After this I was able to export the model as an STL file and upload it to Prusa where I printed it. You can find the stl file linked here on the website. The finished result was pretty cool, and it worked basically exactly how I wanted it to. <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <img src="week5gyroscopethingy.jpg"> </div> <p class="caption">The finished product.</p> <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <video controls> <source src="week5video.mp4" type="video/mp4"> </video> </div> <p class="caption">It moves around and the rings don’t fall out of each other!</p> As far as my plan for my final project goes… doing this documentation with hindsight… I certainly had some lofty ambitions <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <img src="week5plan1.png"> </div> <p class="caption">My Plans</p> <p class="margin"> </p> <p class="margin"> </p> The idea was to make a scanning machine on a gantry and I did sort of accomplish that. While the project didn’t end up looking like this, and certainly the second half about making something that would draw what was being scanned didn’t come true, it’s honestly scary how close the original vision was to what I produced. </div>
